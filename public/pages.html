Project Proposal — Q2 2026

Prepared by Mosiur  ·  April 8, 2026  ·  Confidential

Executive Summary

This proposal outlines the key strategic initiatives planned for Q2 2026, encompassing product expansion, infrastructure improvements, and team scaling. The primary objective is to accelerate user growth by 40% while maintaining the high quality standards that define the mCloud product experience.

The initiatives described herein have been developed in collaboration with cross-functional stakeholders including Engineering, Design, Marketing, and Customer Success. Total estimated investment for Q2 is $240,000, with a projected return of $680,000 in new ARR by end of Q3.

💡 Key Outcome: Deliver mCloud 3.0 by June 30, 2026, with a redesigned core experience, three new integrations, and expanded storage tiers.

Background & Context

mCloud has experienced consistent growth over the past 18 months, with monthly active users increasing from 12,000 to 48,000. However, recent user research has identified friction points in the core workflow — specifically around file organization, cross-device sync reliability, and the onboarding experience for new users.

Competitor analysis conducted in March 2026 revealed that mCloud's current feature set lags behind the market in three areas: collaborative editing, AI-powered search, and mobile-first design. These gaps represent both a risk and an opportunity.

Proposed Initiatives

1. Collaborative Editing (Priority: High)

Introduce real-time collaborative editing across Pages, Numbers, and Keynote. This feature requires significant backend infrastructure investment but represents the single highest-impact improvement based on user demand surveys (68% of respondents cited this as their top request).

  • Real-time cursor presence for up to 12 simultaneous editors
  • Comment threads and @mentions with notification delivery
  • Version history with named checkpoints and branch restoration
  • Permission levels: View, Comment, Edit, Admin

2. AI-Powered Search (Priority: High)

Integrate semantic search capabilities across all mCloud content types. Users will be able to search using natural language queries, with results ranked by relevance, recency, and personal usage patterns. The system will surface documents, files, contacts, and calendar events in a unified search interface.

3. Mobile Experience Redesign (Priority: Medium)

A comprehensive redesign of the iOS and Android applications, moving from the current adapted-desktop approach to a mobile-first interaction paradigm. Key improvements include gesture-based navigation, offline-first architecture, and adaptive layouts that respond to device capabilities.

Timeline & Milestones

  • April 15 — Engineering kickoff and sprint planning
  • May 1 — Alpha release of collaborative editing (internal)
  • May 15 — Beta program launch (500 selected users)
  • June 1 — AI search integration into staging environment
  • June 15 — Public beta for all features
  • June 30 — mCloud 3.0 general availability

Resource Requirements

Successful execution of these initiatives requires the following resources: 3 additional backend engineers (contract), 1 senior iOS developer, expanded AWS infrastructure (estimated $8,000/mo incremental), and a dedicated QA engineer for the beta program. Design capacity is sufficient with current team.

A detailed budget breakdown is available in the accompanying Numbers spreadsheet (Q2 Budget Tracker). Leadership approval is requested by April 12 to maintain the proposed timeline.
